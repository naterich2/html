This whole process has made me think critically about my alcohol use. I always knew making bad decisions is a real risk of getting drunk, but I never thought I got drunk enough to make stupid decisions I have never had an issue with making a bad decision from alcohol like I did that night, and this experience has made me realize it doesn’t have to be a bad decision I make that can end badly, but it can also be a good decision I don’t make that can cause fallout. This realization has definitely been the most impactful on me, because the level of intoxification that can cause me to not make a good decision is much lower than the level that would cause me to make a bad decision. It’s made me realize bad things can happen from a lower intoxification as well as from a higher one. Secondly, I would have guessed the rate of being caught for something when someone is drunk enough to do something that could get them caught would be fairly low. The actual statistic is 17% of students which seems like a very large percentage to me. Maybe this number stems from what I said before, students who are not very drunk, but have lost enough control over themselves to not make a good decision, and end up getting caught for it.
